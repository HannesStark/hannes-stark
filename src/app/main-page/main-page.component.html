GNNs in Reinforcement Learning

Technical University of Munich

Nov 2020 - Mar 2021 Munich, Germany

We used graph representations of robots in reinforcement learning for locomotion problems where the robot has to learn to walk. The idea is that a policy learned by a GNN that captures the structure of the robot will generalize better and enable things like zero-shot transfer to a robot with a different geometry. The results are written up here and the code is available to reproduce everything (and render fun videos of robots learning to walk).
