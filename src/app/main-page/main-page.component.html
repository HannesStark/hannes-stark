Protein Language Models for Protein Prediction

Prof. Burkhard Rost, TUM

Sept 2020 - Feb 2021 Munich, Germany

I developed a new attention mechanism and architecture for predicting proteins' subcellular location beating the previous state-of-the-art by 5 percentage points. I wrote up the results in a paper that is currently under anonymous review at ICML, but you can already look at the code and reproduce all the results. Besides that I evaluated different types of learned representations for proteins and what information is captured by Transformer's protein embeddings, which ultimately led to my method.

Guided Research Computer Vision

Prof. Matthias Nießner, TUM

March 2020 - Sept 2020 Munich, Germany

We used Neural Radiance Fields to render novel views of a scene containing a human. These radiance fields can capture a scene with a low amount of images and then render images from a new unseen viewpoint that did not exist in the original images. Our method added the ability to also change the pose of the human such that you can, for instance, make him walk. Check out the project or the paper .

PROJECTS

GNNs in Reinforcement Learning

Technical University of Munich

Nov 2020 - Mar 2021 Munich, Germany

This project was part of a course. We used graph representations of robots in reinforcement learning for locomotion problems where the robot has to learn to walk. The idea is that a policy learned by a GNN that captures the structure of the robot will generalize better and enable things like zero-shot transfer to a robot with a different geometry. The results are written up here and the code is available to reproduce everything (and render fun videos of robots learning to walk).

Bachelor Thesis: Understanding Variational Autoencoders’ Latent Representations of Remote Sensing Images

Bundeswehr University Munich

May 2019 - Sept 2019 Munich, Germany

My thesis was my first practical introduction to machine learning. I trained a Variational Autoencoder on remote sensing images and visualized the latent space in various ways. Bishop's "Pattern Recognition And Machine Learning" basically saved me and was my main source for learning. It taught me the basics I needed from what neural networks, entropy, or convolutions even are to variational inference and latent variable models. Check out my thesis or have a look at the code .

Tool for calculating Network centralities

Bundeswehr University Munich

I started by investigating the theory on different graph centrality measures in the context of computer networks.
