Master's Thesis

Prof. Pietro Liò, Cambridge University

Feb 2021 - Present Cambridge, United Kingdom

I am working on semi-supervised learning for molecular graphs where we try to leverage 3D structure information of molecules. The current idea is to maximize the mutual information between a spectral representation of the 3D structure and another embedding that is only based on the molecular graph. After this, the learned representations can be used for downstream prediction tasks, or the weights can be finetuned to obtain better predictions for molecular properties that are important in areas like drug discovery. In this project, I am also collaborating with Invivo AI.

IDP Bioinformatics

Rostlab at TUM

Sept 2020 - Feb 2021 Munich, Germany

I developed a new attention mechanism and architecture for predicting proteins' subcellular location beating the previous state-of-the-art by 5 percentage points. I wrote up the results in a paper that is currently under anonymous review at ICML, but you can already look at the code and reproduce all the results. Besides that I evaluated different types of learned representations for proteins and what information is captured by Transformer's protein embeddings, which ultimately led to my method.

Guided Research Computer Vision

Niessnerlab at TUM

March 2020 - Sept 2020 Munich, Germany

We used Neural Radiance Fields to render novel views of a scene containing a human. These radiance fields can capture a scene with a low amount of images and then render images from a new unseen viewpoint that did not exist in the original images.
